Protected Areas and Land Use

Canada's Protected Areas
Source: https://canadiangeographic.ca/articles/infographic-canadas-protected-areas


Protected areas are an essential element of preserving nature in our modern world.


A protected area is defined by the IUCN as
"a clearly defined geographical space, recognised, dedicated and managed, through legal or other effective means, to achieve the long term conservation of nature."
The government of Ontario states that these spaces contain "old-growth forests, lakes, rivers and wetlands, archaeological sites [and] cultural values [and a] habitat for rare or endangered plants and animals." Protected areas are important, because they preserve rich ecosystems and organisms that are threatened by climate change and human interference, as well as protecting Indigenous land and culture.

An image of the Canadian Shield
Source: https://canadiangeographic.ca/articles/behind-the-canadian-shield/

Canada has a long history of protecting local nature, starting in 1885 with Government protection of the Banff Hot Springs. Protected areas face issues such as protecting nearly-extinct species, who need to be carefully bred and protected to ensure the continuation of their species. Additionally, the administration of the area needs to ensure that their activities meet the sensitivities and needs of local indigenous populations. Lastly, the issue of climate change poses a threat to all natural areas, and as such, protected areas have to contend with the issue of a hostile climate. Canada has ancient, beautiful land such as the Canadian Shield, which is the oldest land in North America and hosts vast ecological diversity. Thanks to protected areas, the beauty of the Canadian landscape and the vast cultural importance it holds and the wildlife it contains can be preserved. In conclusion, protected areas are essential to protect Canadian wildlife and nature, and to protect our natural heritage and culture. However, these regions face natural and administrative challenges and issues that must be considered.
